## Delta Packet for the Active Boss Walk

These are only the new decisions or cautions supplied by this research. They
are keyed to the live combined-walk ledger in NedsChorus
`docs/issues/9-neds-notes.md` at commit `66cf592`. Present one item at a time;
the full evidence remains below.

### Outer item 2 — `define-work`

Retain. Add an evidence-bound admission preflight, but do not make it a
universal gate. A reported defect routes to diagnosis; a trivial correction
should not trigger. Do not create a separate `admit-change` unless a combined-
versus-split evaluation proves a real trigger or decision-quality gain.

### Outer item 3 — `plan-rewrite-slice`

Retain. The four-way quarry classification is essential:
`MUST PRESERVE`, `INTENTIONAL CHANGE`, `OLD BUG`, `UNRESOLVED`. Require evidence
and oracle ownership per classification. Parity is a measurement; “zero
differences” is not the goal when old behavior is defective.

### Outer item 4 — `design-change`

Retain the official code-architect spine, but reject prompt-induced certainty.
“Recommend one architecture” applies only after the evidence supports one.
The skill also needs `needs-feasibility-evidence`, `needs-human-decision`,
`postponed`, `rejected`, and `unknown`.

### Outer item 5 — `write-test-plan`

Highest-value missing packaged capability and the leading first dogfood
candidate for the already-designed throat build. The complete proposed
contract is below. Its read-only throat application returned
`needs-design-clarification`, identifying nine missing executable/test
bindings rather than inventing commands. Recent benchmarks reinforce that
generated tests need executed fail-to-pass, fault-detection, preservation, and
repository-reach evidence; coverage and plausibility are insufficient.

### Outer item 6 — `attack-artifact`

Do not admit separately yet. It overlaps heavily with the required boot
`d-review`. First compare trimmed `d-review`, that trim plus isolation/intent-
gaming/clean-result deltas, and a separate skill. Remove the quarry rule to
report uncertain LOW findings and leave filtering to the reader.

### Outer item 7 — `implement-with-evidence`

Retain only the evidence kernel: observe a check fail for the intended reason,
implement the bounded behavior, run relevant regressions, inspect clean output.
The witness may be a test, build, script, or repro. Reject deletion mandates and
allow a recorded exception when a pre-change witness is impossible.

### Outer item 8 — `diagnose-failure`

Retain the causal loop and the mitigation/root-cause distinction. Three failed
fixes is an escalation warning, not permission for three guesses and not proof
that architecture is wrong. Every edit needs new discriminating evidence;
`unknown` is preferable to a manufactured fix.

### Outer item 9 — `review-change`

Retain as a distinct exact-revision skill. Add first-pass independence before
reading other reviewers. A finding must be introduced, meaningful,
demonstrable, discrete, and actionable; clean results are valid. Do not merge
this with design/test-plan attack merely to reduce skill count.

### Outer item 10 — `eval-agent-change`

Retain the lean method, not Waza as a platform. Use cold no-skill/prior
baselines, near-miss triggers, frozen assertions, production runtimes,
ablations, repeated raw runs, and human judgment. Report raw counts; never
average away an essential safety or intent failure.

### Outer item 11 — official skill-creator extraction

Adopt progressive disclosure and description-as-routing-interface. Treat
“pushy” descriptions as a Claude-specific hypothesis requiring false-trigger
tests. Reject 100–200 words as a portable target: the common spec caps the
field at 1,024 characters, and both runtimes may truncate listings. Front-load
a concise positive and negative scope, then measure. Subjective outputs still
need evaluation of triggers, process obligations, forbidden actions,
completeness, and consistency; only final quality judgment necessarily remains
human.

### Outer item 12 — boot-set roll-up

Keep the five founding skills. Do not add the whole candidate lifecycle. Select
one candidate from the first real task's missing decision. On the current
throat task, `write-test-plan` has the strongest fit; it should be one
`SKILL.md` with manual evaluation, no helper framework.
